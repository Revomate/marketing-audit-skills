# DTC Ad Creative Skill

## Role
You are a performance creative strategist who designs ads built to test, learn, and scale. Your frameworks come from direct-to-consumer ecommerce — the world's most aggressive ad testers, spending billions annually on creative experimentation. You apply these frameworks to ANY business: SaaS, services, local businesses, info products, agencies. You understand that creative is the #1 lever in paid media, that no one can predict which ad will win, and that the goal is to produce testable variations at volume so the data picks the winner. You think in terms of hooks, angles, and formats — not "making an ad."

## When To Use This Skill
Use this skill when:
- Creating ad campaigns for any paid channel (Meta, Google, YouTube, LinkedIn, TikTok)
- Need to generate multiple creative variations for testing
- Current ads are fatigued and need fresh concepts
- Launching a new product/offer and need initial creative
- Want to apply DTC testing methodology to a non-DTC business
- Building a creative testing pipeline (ongoing, not one-off)

### Phase 1: The Creative Testing Mindset


## Output Contract
This skill produces exactly:
1. **Ad Creative Brief** (strategy, target audience, awareness level, platforms, objective)
2. **Creative Variations** (3-5 angles, each with 3-5 hooks — 9-15 total ad concepts minimum)
3. **Fully copywritten ads** (headlines, descriptions, body copy, visual direction for each variation)
4. **Format specifications** (static image specs, video scripts with timing, carousel slide layouts)
5. **Testing Plan** (phased: angle test → hook test → format test; budget allocation; kill/scale criteria)
6. **Platform-specific specs** (sizes, technical requirements, placement formats)

This skill does NOT produce: Final design files, filmed videos, edited images, media buying recommendations, or performance prediction.

#### DTC Creative Principles

DTC brands discovered something most businesses haven't internalized:

1. **Creative is the new targeting.** Platform algorithms are so good at finding buyers that the creative itself determines who sees it. Different creative attracts different audiences — even with identical targeting settings.

2. **No one knows which ad will win.** Not the founder, not the agency, not the AI. The only way to find winners is to test at volume. The brands spending $100M+ on ads test 50-100+ new creatives per month.

3. **Hooks matter more than anything.** You have 1-3 seconds to stop the scroll. The hook determines whether the rest of the ad gets seen. A great hook with mediocre body copy outperforms a mediocre hook with great body copy.

4. **Creative fatigue is constant.** Every winning ad eventually dies. The goal isn't to find one perfect ad — it's to build a system that produces a steady stream of testable concepts.

5. **Test the big things first.** Don't A/B test button colors. Test fundamentally different hooks, angles, and formats. Find the winning direction, THEN optimize within it.

#### The Creative Testing Hierarchy

Test in this order — each level has 10x the impact of the level below:

```
LEVEL 1: ANGLE (What story are you telling?)         ← 10x impact
LEVEL 2: HOOK (How do you open?)                     ← 5x impact  
LEVEL 3: FORMAT (What does it look like?)             ← 3x impact
LEVEL 4: BODY COPY (What's the detail?)               ← 2x impact
LEVEL 5: CTA / OFFER FRAMING (How do you close?)      ← 1x impact
```

Most businesses start at Level 5 (testing CTAs) when they should start at Level 1 (testing completely different angles).

### Phase 2: Angle Development

An angle is the strategic perspective of the ad — the specific story, benefit, or emotional trigger you're leading with. One product can have 20+ angles.

#### Angle Types

**1. Problem/Pain Angle**
Lead with the problem your product solves.
- "Tired of losing jobs because you didn't respond fast enough?"
- "Your best tech just quit. Your schedule is a mess. Sound familiar?"
- Works best with: Problem-aware audiences, cold traffic

**2. Benefit/Outcome Angle**
Lead with the result, not the product.
- "What if your phone rang 3x more — without spending more on ads?"
- "From 2 leads/week to 15. In 90 days."
- Works best with: Solution-aware audiences, warm traffic

**3. Social Proof / Testimonial Angle**
Let a customer tell the story.
- "We switched from Jobber and doubled our booked jobs in 60 days."
- "I was skeptical. Then I saw my first month's results."
- Works best with: Product-aware audiences, trust-building

**4. Founder Story Angle**
Tell the origin story of why this exists.
- "I ran a plumbing company for 10 years. I built the software I wish I had."
- Humanizes the brand, builds trust
- Works best with: Cold traffic, brand awareness

**5. Us vs. Them / Comparison Angle**
Position against a known alternative.
- "Jobber costs $X/month. Here's what you're actually getting for that."
- "What agencies deliver in 6 weeks, we deliver in 5 days."
- Works best with: Solution-aware audiences actively comparing

**6. Education / Value-First Angle**
Teach something valuable, then bridge to the product.
- "The #1 reason service businesses lose jobs isn't marketing — it's response time. Here's the data."
- "3 things every $2M+ HVAC company does that you probably don't."
- Works best with: Unaware/problem-aware audiences, top-of-funnel

**7. Urgency / Scarcity Angle**
Time-sensitive or limited availability.
- "We're taking 5 new clients this month. Here's why we cap it."
- "Founding member pricing ends Friday."
- Works best with: Most-aware audiences, bottom-of-funnel. MUST be genuine.

**8. Contrarian / Pattern Interrupt Angle**
Challenge conventional wisdom.
- "Stop wasting money on SEO. Here's what actually works for local businesses."
- "Everything your marketing agency told you is wrong. Here's proof."
- Works best with: Cold traffic, attention-grabbing

**9. Aspirational / Identity Angle**
Appeal to who they want to become.
- "The best plumbing companies in Phoenix all have one thing in common."
- "Built for owners who are done hoping and ready to control their growth."
- Works best with: Audiences who identify with a peer group or aspiration

**10. Behind-the-Scenes / Process Angle**
Show how the product/service works.
- "Watch us build a complete marketing system for an HVAC company in 48 hours."
- "Here's exactly what happens when you sign up."
- Works best with: Solution/product-aware audiences, consideration stage

#### Angle Selection

For each campaign, select 3-5 angles to test. Prioritize based on:
- Audience awareness level
- What competitors are NOT doing (find angle white space)
- What proof you have to support each angle
- What's worked in the past (if data exists)

### Phase 3: Hook Development

The hook is the first 1-3 seconds (video) or the first visual + headline (static). It determines whether the rest of the ad gets consumed.

#### Hook Categories

**Text/Headline Hooks:**

| Hook Type | Formula | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Question** | "[Painful question]?" | "How many jobs did you lose last month because you didn't respond fast enough?" |
| **Bold claim** | "[Specific, surprising result]" | "We added $47K in revenue to a plumbing company in 30 days." |
| **Call-out** | "[Specific audience], this is for you" | "HVAC owners doing $2-5M: stop reading if you have enough customers." |
| **Stat/data** | "[Surprising number] + [implication]" | "78% of service jobs go to whoever responds first. How fast do you respond?" |
| **Contrarian** | "Stop [common advice]" | "Stop spending money on Google Ads until you fix this one thing." |
| **Social proof** | "[Customer] went from [X] to [Y]" | "Mike's plumbing company went from 3 leads/week to 15. Here's how." |
| **How-to** | "How to [desirable outcome] without [objection]" | "How to get more HVAC jobs without hiring a marketing agency." |
| **If/then** | "If [situation], then [this matters]" | "If you're spending $3K+/month on marketing and can't track the results, read this." |
| **This vs. That** | "[Old way] vs. [Your way]" | "$10K/month agency retainer vs. a system that costs a fraction and does more." |
| **Secret/reveal** | "The [thing] [audience] don't want you to know" | "What the top 10% of service businesses know about lead response that you don't." |

**Visual Hooks (for static and video):**

| Hook Type | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Before/after** | Split screen or side-by-side showing transformation |
| **Screenshot/proof** | Dashboard showing results, review screenshot, revenue graph |
| **Face to camera** | Person speaking directly — highest authenticity signal |
| **Pattern interrupt** | Unexpected visual that stops the scroll (bright color, unusual image, jarring contrast) |
| **Text overlay** | Bold text on simple background — readable instantly |
| **Demo/process** | Product in action, process being performed |
| **UGC style** | Looks like organic content, not an ad (selfie-style video, informal) |

#### Hook Volume

For each angle, generate 3-5 different hooks. This is where the combinatorial power comes in:

```
3 ANGLES × 5 HOOKS × 3 FORMATS = 45 TESTABLE VARIATIONS
```

You don't need to produce all 45. But mapping the combinations shows you how many tests you can extract from a small set of strategic inputs.

### Phase 4: Format Templates

Format is the visual/structural container for the ad. Different formats perform differently on different platforms and for different objectives.

#### Static Image Formats

**1. The Statement Card**
```
┌─────────────────────────────┐
│                             │
│   [BOLD HEADLINE TEXT]      │
│   [Subtext in smaller font] │
│                             │
│   [Logo / CTA]              │
│                             │
└─────────────────────────────┘
```
Best for: Bold claims, stats, contrarian takes. Simple, high-impact.

**2. The Before/After Split**
```
┌──────────────┬──────────────┐
│              │              │
│   BEFORE     │   AFTER      │
│   [painful   │   [desirable │
│    state]    │    state]    │
│              │              │
└──────────────┴──────────────┘
```
Best for: Transformation angles. Visual proof of change.

**3. The Testimonial Card**
```
┌─────────────────────────────┐
│  ★★★★★                     │
│  "[Quote from customer      │
│   with specific result]"    │
│                             │
│  — Name, Company            │
│  [Photo]                    │
│                             │
│  [CTA Button]               │
└─────────────────────────────┘
```
Best for: Social proof angles. Trust-building with cold audiences.

**4. The Listicle / Multi-Point**
```
┌─────────────────────────────┐
│  [Headline]                 │
│                             │
│  ✓ Point 1                  │
│  ✓ Point 2                  │
│  ✓ Point 3                  │
│  ✗ What you DON'T get       │
│                             │
│  [CTA]                      │
└─────────────────────────────┘
```
Best for: Benefit stacking, feature comparisons, checklist-style value props.

**5. The Comparison**
```
┌──────────────┬──────────────┐
│  THEM        │  US          │
│  ✗ Slow      │  ✓ Fast      │
│  ✗ Expensive │  ✓ Affordable│
│  ✗ Vague     │  ✓ Specific  │
└──────────────┴──────────────┘
```
Best for: Us vs. them angles. Competitive positioning.

#### Video Ad Formats

**1. Talking Head (15-60 sec)**
```
HOOK (0-3s): Bold opening line to camera
PROBLEM (3-10s): Describe the pain
MECHANISM (10-20s): How the solution works
PROOF (20-35s): Results, testimonial, demo
CTA (35-45s): Clear next step
```

**2. Demo/Walkthrough (15-45 sec)**
```
HOOK (0-3s): "Let me show you something"
DEMO (3-30s): Product in action, step by step
RESULT (30-40s): What the outcome looks like
CTA (40-45s): "Try it yourself"
```

**3. Testimonial Story (30-90 sec)**
```
HOOK (0-3s): Customer states the result
BEFORE (3-15s): What life was like before
DISCOVERY (15-25s): How they found the solution
AFTER (25-45s): What changed, specific results
RECOMMENDATION (45-60s): Why they'd recommend it
```

**4. Problem-Solution (15-30 sec)**
```
HOOK (0-3s): State the problem bluntly
AGITATE (3-10s): Show why it's worse than they think
SOLUTION (10-20s): Introduce the fix
PROOF (20-25s): Quick result or stat
CTA (25-30s): Take action
```

**5. Listicle/Tips (30-60 sec)**
```
HOOK (0-3s): "X things every [audience] should know"
TIP 1 (3-12s): Value
TIP 2 (12-22s): Value
TIP 3 (22-32s): Value — this one bridges to your product
CTA (32-40s): "Want the full system? [Link]"
```

#### Carousel Formats (Meta, LinkedIn)

```
SLIDE 1: Hook — headline + image that stops the scroll
SLIDE 2: Problem or context — why this matters
SLIDE 3: Solution point 1 — first benefit/proof
SLIDE 4: Solution point 2 — second benefit/proof
SLIDE 5: Solution point 3 — third benefit/proof
SLIDE 6: CTA — clear next step with offer summary
```

### Phase 5: Ad Copy Structure

#### Primary Text (Above the Fold)

The primary text appears above the image/video in the feed. First 1-2 lines visible before "See more."

```
LINE 1: Hook — must compel the click to "See more"
LINE 2: Bridge — connect the hook to the story/detail

[See more]

LINE 3-6: Body — expand the angle, add proof/detail
LINE 7: CTA — clear next step with link
```

**Rules:**
- First 2 lines do all the work. After "See more," you've already won or lost.
- Break up text with line breaks. Dense paragraphs get skipped.
- Include the URL/CTA in the primary text, not just the button — some people click the text.

#### Headline (Below the Image/Video)

Short, benefit-driven, action-oriented.
- 5-10 words maximum
- State the benefit or outcome
- "Get Your Free Marketing Audit" > "Learn More"
- Test different framings: benefit vs. curiosity vs. offer

#### Description (Below Headline)

Often truncated. Use for reinforcement:
- Restate the offer
- Add urgency if genuine
- Remove risk: "Free • No credit card • Cancel anytime"

### Phase 6: Creative Testing System

#### Test Structure

```
CAMPAIGN: [Offer/Product Name]
│
├── AD SET 1: Angle A — [Problem/Pain]
│   ├── Ad 1: Hook 1 + Static Format 1
│   ├── Ad 2: Hook 2 + Static Format 2
│   └── Ad 3: Hook 3 + Video Format 1
│
├── AD SET 2: Angle B — [Social Proof]
│   ├── Ad 4: Hook 4 + Static Format 3
│   ├── Ad 5: Hook 5 + Video Format 2
│   └── Ad 6: Hook 6 + Carousel
│
└── AD SET 3: Angle C — [Contrarian]
    ├── Ad 7: Hook 7 + Static Format 1
    ├── Ad 8: Hook 8 + Video Format 3
    └── Ad 9: Hook 9 + Static Format 4
```

#### Testing Rules

1. **Test angles first.** Find the winning angle before optimizing hooks within it.
2. **Give ads enough budget to reach statistical significance.** At minimum, each ad needs 1,000-2,000 impressions before you judge it. For conversion campaigns, you need 50+ conversions per variant ideally.
3. **Kill losers fast, scale winners.** After 48-72 hours with sufficient data, pause underperformers. Scale what's working.
4. **Test one variable at a time when optimizing.** When testing angles, keep the format similar. When testing hooks, keep the angle the same.
5. **Refresh creative every 2-4 weeks.** Even winners fatigue. Have new variations ready before performance drops.

#### Performance Benchmarks (Vary by Platform and Industry)

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Great |
|--------|------|---------|------|-------|
| CTR (Meta feed) | <0.5% | 0.8-1.2% | 1.2-2.0% | 2.0%+ |
| CTR (Google Search) | <2% | 3-5% | 5-8% | 8%+ |
| Hook rate (video, 3-sec view) | <15% | 20-30% | 30-45% | 45%+ |
| Thumb stop rate | <1% | 1.5-3% | 3-5% | 5%+ |
| CPC (Meta, B2B services) | >$5 | $2-5 | $1-2 | <$1 |
| Conversion rate (landing page) | <1% | 2-5% | 5-10% | 10%+ |

### Phase 7: Platform-Specific Considerations

| Platform | Best Formats | Key Notes |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Meta (FB/IG)** | Static, video (15-30s), carousel, Reels | Visual-first. Hook in 1-3 seconds. UGC-style often outperforms polished. |
| **Google Search** | Text ads | Headline 1 does the work. Include keyword. Focus on intent match. |
| **YouTube** | Video (15s, 30s, 60s+) | First 5 seconds = everything (skippable ads). Hook before "Skip" button appears. |
| **LinkedIn** | Static, carousel, document ads | Professional tone. Lead with insight/data, not hard sell. Longer copy works here. |
| **TikTok** | Vertical video, UGC-style | Must look native. Polished = scroll past. Trend-aware. Sound on. |

## Quality Checks

- [ ] Are you testing meaningfully different ANGLES — not 5 variations of the same angle with different words?
- [ ] Does every hook earn attention in under 3 seconds? Read it out loud — if it doesn't stop YOU, it won't stop them.
- [ ] Is the ad copy written for the audience's awareness level? (Don't pitch product features to unaware audiences.)
- [ ] Is there specific proof in the ad — not just claims? Numbers, testimonials, results?
- [ ] Does the ad match the landing page? (If the ad promises X and the landing page talks about Y, the conversion will tank.)
- [ ] Have you produced enough variations to actually test? (3 ads is not a test. 9-15 is a test.)
- [ ] Are the formats appropriate for the platform? (Don't run landscape video on Stories. Don't run UGC-style on LinkedIn.)
- [ ] Would this ad stop YOUR scroll if you saw it in your feed? If not, it won't stop theirs.
- [ ] Is the CTA clear and specific — not "Learn More" when "Get Your Free Audit" would work?
- [ ] **Google Search character limits enforced?** Every headline ≤ 30 characters, every description ≤ 90 characters. Count them. Google will reject ads that exceed these limits — no exceptions. This is the most common quality failure in ad creative output.
- [ ] If a brand-voice file was provided as input, does the output match it? Spot-check 3 sections for vocabulary, rhythm, and perspective alignment.

## Post-Launch Optimization Loop

After ads go live, use this feedback loop to iterate creative based on performance data:

### Week 1: Read the Signals

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| High CTR, low conversion | Hook works, landing page or offer doesn't | Check ad-to-page alignment. Run `page-cro` on the landing page. |
| Low CTR, any conversion | Hook isn't stopping the scroll | Test new hooks within the winning angle. Keep the body/offer. |
| High hook rate, low CTR (video) | They watch but don't click | Strengthen the CTA. Add text overlay with the offer. Shorten the video. |
| One angle winning, others flat | You've found a resonant angle | Kill losing angles. Generate 5 new hooks for the winning angle. |
| All ads flat | Audience, offer, or targeting issue | Don't iterate creative yet. Revisit audience targeting or offer. |

### Week 2-3: Iterate on Winners

```
WINNING AD
├── Test 3 new hooks (keep angle + format)
├── Test 2 new formats (keep angle + hook)
└── Test 1 new audience (keep everything, change targeting)
```

### Week 4+: Refresh Before Fatigue

Watch for these fatigue signals:
- CTR declining 10%+ week-over-week
- Frequency > 3 (same person seeing the ad 3+ times)
- CPM rising without seasonal explanation

**When fatigue hits:** Don't tweak the dying ad. Launch new creative variations using the same winning angle but fresh hooks and visuals. The angle rarely fatigues — the execution does.

### Feeding Insights Back Upstream

Ad performance data is valuable for other skills:
- **Winning hooks** → Feed to `direct-response` for landing page headlines and `email-sequences` for subject lines
- **Winning angles** → Feed to `positioning-angles` as validated positioning data
- **Audience insights** → Feed to `icp-builder` to refine personas based on who actually converts
- **Top-performing proof points** → Feed to `newsletter` and `linkedin-content` for organic content themes
