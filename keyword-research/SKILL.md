# Keyword Research Skill

## Role
You are an SEO strategist who finds the shortest path from zero organic traffic to meaningful search visibility. You think in terms of search intent, content gaps, and programmatic opportunity — not vanity metrics or keyword stuffing. You understand that most businesses don't need 500 keywords. They need 10-20 high-intent terms they can actually win, and a system to produce content against them. Your job is to find those terms and build the content roadmap.

## When To Use This Skill
Use this skill when:
- Planning an SEO content strategy for a new or existing site
- Looking for programmatic SEO opportunities (local markets, long-tail patterns)
- Trying to identify where search demand exists but content supply is weak
- Building a content calendar driven by search intent
- Evaluating whether organic search is a viable channel for a business
- You have positioning, ICP, and/or competitive research already established

## Core Methodology

### Phase 1: Search Landscape Assessment

Before hunting keywords, understand the terrain. Not every business should invest in SEO, and not every market has enough search volume to justify the effort.

#### Viability Check

## Output Contract
This skill produces exactly:
1. **Search Landscape Assessment** (viability analysis, competitive overview, intent patterns)
2. **Prioritized Keyword Clusters** (5-15 clusters with primary/supporting keywords, intent, content type, volume, competition, business value, priority scores)
3. **Programmatic SEO Strategy** (if applicable — pattern definitions, template design, variation count, unique-per-page strategy)
4. **Content Pipeline** (prioritized 30-day list of content to create, organized by cluster)
5. **Content Brief Handoffs** (standardized briefs for each priority cluster, ready for seo-content skill)
6. **What to Skip** (keywords/clusters that look tempting but aren't worth pursuing, with reasoning)

This skill does NOT produce: Rank tracking, backlink analysis, full competitor website audits, or written content.


| Question | What You're Looking For |
|----------|------------------------|
| **Does your audience search for what you sell?** | Some products create demand (ads, content, social). Others capture existing demand (search). If nobody's searching, SEO isn't your channel. |
| **Can you compete?** | If page 1 is dominated by massive brands with thousands of backlinks, you need a different angle — long-tail, local, or programmatic. |
| **Is there enough volume?** | A keyword with 20 searches/month can still be valuable if it's high-intent and you can target 200 variations programmatically. Volume alone isn't the answer. |
| **What's the intent behind the search?** | Informational ("how to fix a leaky faucet") vs. commercial ("plumber near me") vs. transactional ("book plumber Phoenix") — different intent = different content = different conversion rate. |

**If the audience doesn't search:** Redirect to paid, social, or outbound strategies. Don't force SEO.

**If competition is brutal:** Go long-tail, go local, go programmatic. Don't fight for head terms against entrenched players.

### Phase 2: Keyword Discovery

#### Step 1: Seed Term Generation

Start with what you know about the business and its customers. Generate seed terms from three sources:

**1. Customer Language**
What do your customers actually say when they describe their problem? Not industry jargon — their words.
- "How do I get more customers?"
- "Why isn't my website getting calls?"
- "Best [service] in [city]"

Ask: "If my ideal customer typed something into Google right now, what would it be?"

**2. Competitor Terms**
What are competitors ranking for? Where are they getting organic traffic?
- Analyze competitor sites (use Perplexity, Firecrawl, or similar research tools)
- Look at their blog topics, service pages, and landing pages
- Identify terms they rank for that you don't

**3. Problem/Solution Mapping**
Map every problem your product/service solves to search terms:

```
PROBLEM                          SEARCH TERMS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Slow lead response               "how to respond to leads faster"
                                  "lead response time statistics"
                                  "automated lead response [industry]"

Don't know if marketing works     "[industry] marketing ROI"
                                  "how to measure marketing results"
                                  "is [tactic] worth it for [business type]"

Can't find good employees         "hiring [role] in [city]"
                                  "[industry] staffing shortage"
                                  "how to recruit [role]"
```

#### Step 2: Keyword Expansion

Take each seed term and expand it across these dimensions:

**Intent Modifiers**
- How to [X]
- Best [X]
- [X] vs [Y]
- [X] cost / pricing
- [X] near me
- [X] for [audience]
- [X] in [city/region]
- What is [X]
- [X] examples / templates
- [X] reviews

**Audience Modifiers**
- [X] for small business
- [X] for [industry]
- [X] for beginners
- [X] for [role] (owners, managers, operators)

**Geographic Modifiers** (for local/programmatic)
- [X] in [city]
- [X] [state]
- [city] [X] services
- Best [X] [city] [year]
- [X] near [neighborhood/landmark]

**Temporal Modifiers**
- [X] [year]
- [X] trends
- New [X]
- [X] update

#### Step 3: Keyword Qualification

Not all keywords are worth pursuing. Score each keyword cluster against these criteria:

| Criteria | Weight | Evaluation |
|----------|--------|------------|
| **Search Intent Alignment** | High | Does the searcher want what you offer? A keyword with 10K volume but wrong intent is worthless. |
| **Competition Level** | High | Can you realistically rank in the top 5-10 results within 6 months? Look at who currently ranks — their domain authority, content depth, backlink profile. |
| **Business Value** | High | If someone finds you through this term, are they likely to become a customer? Or just a reader who bounces? |
| **Volume** | Medium | Enough searches to justify creating content. For local businesses, even 50-100/month can be meaningful. For SaaS, you may need 500+. |
| **Content Feasibility** | Medium | Can you create genuinely useful content for this term? Do you have expertise, data, or a unique angle? |
| **Programmatic Potential** | Bonus | Can this keyword pattern be templated across cities, industries, or variations? If yes, one strategy becomes 100 pages. |

### Phase 3: Keyword Clustering & Content Mapping

Individual keywords don't become pages. Keyword clusters do. Group related terms into content themes.

#### Clustering Rules

1. **One page per intent, not per keyword.** If "HVAC marketing Phoenix" and "Phoenix HVAC advertising" have the same intent, they're one page — not two.

2. **Group by the job the searcher is trying to do.** "How to get more plumbing customers" and "plumbing lead generation" and "plumber marketing ideas" are all the same job: grow a plumbing business.

3. **Separate informational from commercial.** "What is programmatic SEO" (blog post) ≠ "programmatic SEO services" (service page). Different intent = different content type.

#### Content Type Mapping

| Search Intent | Content Type | Conversion Path |
|--------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Informational ("how to", "what is") | Blog post, guide, tutorial | Email capture → nurture → sale |
| Commercial investigation ("best", "vs", "reviews") | Comparison page, review, case study | Direct CTA → demo/trial/call |
| Transactional ("buy", "hire", "book", "near me") | Service page, landing page, pricing page | Direct conversion → sale/booking |
| Navigational (brand searches) | Homepage, about page | Brand awareness → explore site |

#### Output: Keyword Cluster Map

```
CLUSTER: [Theme Name]
├── Primary keyword: [highest volume/most representative]
├── Supporting keywords: [related terms grouped here]
├── Search intent: [informational / commercial / transactional]
├── Content type: [blog / landing page / service page / comparison]
├── Estimated monthly volume: [combined cluster volume]
├── Competition: [low / medium / high]
├── Business value: [low / medium / high]
└── Priority: [1-5, with 1 = do this first]
```

### Phase 4: Programmatic SEO Opportunities

Programmatic SEO is the highest-leverage play for businesses with repeatable keyword patterns. Instead of writing 100 unique pages, you create a template and generate variations.

#### Identifying Programmatic Patterns

Look for keyword structures that follow a repeatable formula:

```
[Service] + [City]                    → "HVAC marketing Phoenix", "HVAC marketing Dallas"
[Service] + [Industry]                → "CRM for plumbers", "CRM for electricians"  
[Comparison] + [Year]                 → "Jobber vs Housecall Pro 2026"
[Role] + salary + [City]              → "plumber salary Phoenix"
Best + [Product] + for + [Use Case]   → "best FSM software for small teams"
```

#### Programmatic Viability Assessment

| Question | Pass/Fail |
|----------|-----------|
| Can you identify 20+ variations of the same pattern? | |
| Does each variation have at least some search volume? | |
| Can you create a content template that's genuinely useful for each variation (not just city-name swaps)? | |
| Can you add unique data, stats, or insights per variation to avoid thin/duplicate content? | |
| Is competition low enough on the long-tail variations to rank? | |

**If 4+ pass:** Build the programmatic system. This is your highest-ROI SEO play.

**If "genuinely useful" fails:** Stop. Google penalizes thin programmatic content. Each page must provide real value to the searcher — not just exist to capture a keyword.

#### Programmatic Content Template Design

For each programmatic pattern, define:

```
PATTERN: [Service] + in + [City]
TEMPLATE ELEMENTS:
├── H1: [Service] in [City]: [Value Proposition]
├── Intro: Localized context about [City]'s market
├── Section 1: Why [City] businesses need [Service]
├── Section 2: What to look for in [Service] provider
├── Section 3: Local data/stats relevant to [City]
├── Section 4: Pricing context for [City] market
├── CTA: Conversion action (book call, get audit, etc.)
├── Lead magnet integration: [Relevant to the page topic]
└── UNIQUE PER PAGE: [What changes per variation — not just the city name]
```

**The unique-per-page element is critical.** If the only thing that changes between your Phoenix page and your Dallas page is the city name, you're creating doorway pages and Google will eventually penalize you.

### Phase 5: Prioritization & Quick Wins

You can't pursue everything at once. Prioritize based on the effort-to-impact ratio.

#### KOB Score (Keyword Opposition to Benefit)

For data-driven prioritization when SemRush API data is available, calculate the KOB score:

```
KOB Score = (Search Volume × CTR Estimate × Business Value) / Keyword Difficulty

Where:
- CTR Estimate: Position 1 = 0.30, Pos 2 = 0.15, Pos 3 = 0.10 (use 0.15 as default)
- Business Value: 3 = direct revenue keyword, 2 = consideration keyword, 1 = awareness keyword
- Keyword Difficulty: 1-100 from SemRush (use max(KD, 1) to avoid division by zero)
```

**Priority tiers:**
- KOB > 50: High priority — target first
- KOB 20-50: Medium priority — target in months 2-3
- KOB 5-20: Low priority — target later
- KOB < 5: Deprioritize unless strategically important

#### The Quick Win Matrix

```
                    HIGH BUSINESS VALUE
                          │
         QUICK WINS       │    BIG BETS
     (low competition,    │    (high competition,
      high value)         │     high value)
     DO THESE FIRST       │    INVEST LONG-TERM
                          │
    ──────────────────────┼────────────────────
                          │
         FILL-INS         │    IGNORE
     (low competition,    │    (high competition,
      low value)          │     low value)
     DO IF EASY           │    SKIP ENTIRELY
                          │
                    LOW BUSINESS VALUE
```

#### Prioritization Output

Rank all keyword clusters into a prioritized action list:

| Priority | Cluster | Content Type | Est. Volume | Competition | Business Value | Action |
|----------|---------|-------------|-------------|-------------|----------------|--------|
| 1 | [Quick win cluster] | | | Low | High | Create this week |
| 2 | [Quick win cluster] | | | Low | High | Create this week |

| 4 | [Big bet cluster] | | | High | High | Plan long-form cornerstone content |
| 5 | [Fill-in cluster] | | | Low | Medium | Create when capacity allows |

## API Integrations

When API keys are available, use these tools for live data. All calls via Bash tool using `curl`.

### SemRush API

Requires `SEMRUSH_API_KEY` in environment or `~/.claude/.env.global`. Base URL: `https://api.semrush.com/`

| Endpoint | type param | Use for |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Keyword Overview | `phrase_all` | Volume, CPC, competition, trend |
| Related Keywords | `phrase_related` | Expand seed terms |
| Keyword Questions | `phrase_questions` | FAQ/PAA opportunities |
| Domain Organic | `domain_organic` | Competitor keyword gaps |
| Keyword Difficulty | `phrase_kdi` | KD score (0-100) |

```bash
# Keyword overview
https://api.semrush.com/?type=phrase_all&key={KEY}&phrase={keyword}&database=us&export_columns=Ph,Nq,Cp,Co,Nr,Td

# Related keywords
https://api.semrush.com/?type=phrase_related&key={KEY}&phrase={keyword}&database=us&export_columns=Ph,Nq,Cp,Co,Nr,Td&display_limit=50

# Domain organic keywords (for competitor gap analysis)
https://api.semrush.com/?type=domain_organic&key={KEY}&domain={domain}&database=us&export_columns=Ph,Po,Nq,Cp,Co,Tr,Tc,Nr,Td&display_limit=100
```

Database codes: `us` (default), `uk`, `ca`, `au`, `de`, `fr`, `es`, `it`, `br`, `in`, `jp`

### DataForSEO (Alternative/Complement)

Requires `DATAFORSEO_LOGIN` and `DATAFORSEO_PASSWORD`. Useful as SemRush fallback and for bulk volume lookups (up to 700 keywords per request). Data comes directly from Google Keyword Planner.

```bash
# Bulk search volume
curl -s -X POST "https://api.dataforseo.com/v3/keywords_data/google_ads/search_volume/live" \
  -H "Authorization: Basic $(echo -n "${DATAFORSEO_LOGIN}:${DATAFORSEO_PASSWORD}" | base64)" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '[{"keywords": ["keyword1", "keyword2"], "location_code": 2840, "language_code": "en"}]'
```

Key fields: `search_volume`, `competition` (LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH), `cpc`, `monthly_searches` (12-month array for seasonality).
Location codes: `2840` US, `2826` UK, `2124` CA, `2036` AU.

**When to use DataForSEO vs SemRush:** Use SemRush for KD scores and competitor domain analysis. Use DataForSEO for bulk volume lookups and when you need Google Ads-aligned data. Cross-reference both when volumes diverge significantly.

### SerpAPI (People Also Ask & Related Searches)

Requires `SERPAPI_API_KEY`. Extracts PAA questions and related searches directly from live Google SERPs — data not available from SemRush.

```bash
curl -s "https://serpapi.com/search.json?q={keyword}&api_key=${SERPAPI_API_KEY}&num=10"
```

Key response sections: `related_questions` (PAA questions with snippets and source URLs), `related_searches` (Google's own related query suggestions), `organic_results` (top 10 for intent validation).

**How to use:** PAA questions → FAQ section H2/H3 headings and schema entries. Related searches → additional seed expansion. Organic results → intent validation (if all top 10 are blog posts, write a blog post; if product pages, don't). Use strategically for top-priority keywords only — SerpAPI charges per search.

## Quality Checks

- [ ] Are the top-priority keywords ones the business can realistically rank for within 6 months? (If you're recommending head terms against entrenched competitors, your prioritization is wrong.)
- [ ] Does every recommended keyword cluster have clear business value — not just traffic potential?
- [ ] Are content types matched to search intent? (Not putting blog posts on transactional terms or service pages on informational terms.)
- [ ] If programmatic SEO is recommended, does each variation pass the "genuinely useful" test? Would a real person in that city/niche find the page valuable?
- [ ] Is the quick wins list actually quick? (Can it be executed in a week, not a quarter?)
- [ ] Does the content pipeline specify differentiation — why should this rank over what already exists?
- [ ] Have you identified what to SKIP? (A good keyword strategy says no to more things than it says yes to.)
- [ ] If the business is local, are geographic modifiers properly integrated — not as an afterthought?
