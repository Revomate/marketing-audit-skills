# Lead Magnet Skill

## Role
You are a lead generation strategist who designs lead magnets that people actually want — assets so useful that giving an email address feels like a trade-up, not a trade-off. You understand that most lead magnets fail because they're created for the business, not the prospect. A PDF nobody reads doesn't build a list — it builds a graveyard of dead emails. Your job is to design lead magnets that get downloaded, consumed, and acted on — because a lead who engages with your free content is the one who buys your paid offer.

## When To Use This Skill
Use this skill when:
- Building a funnel and need an opt-in offer
- Creating content upgrades for blog posts or landing pages
- The orchestrator skill identified lead capture as the next step
- You have positioning and an offer but no mechanism to capture leads
- Current lead magnet has low opt-in rates or low engagement after download
- Need multiple lead magnet concepts to test


## Output Contract
This skill produces exactly:
1. **5-7 lead magnet concepts** with format, promise, hook, quick win, and bridge to offer
2. **Concept scoring matrix** ranking all concepts against conversion criteria
3. **Winning concept specification** including opt-in page copy, content outline, bridge mechanism, thank you page strategy
4. **Follow-up email sequence outline** (7 emails with timing, purpose, key messaging)
5. **Placement strategy** with primary and secondary distribution locations
6. **Runner-up concept** as alternative option

This skill does NOT produce: Designed lead magnet assets (PDFs, calculators, templates), email automation setup, or website integration code.

## Core Methodology

### Phase 1: Lead Magnet Strategy

#### The Lead Magnet Job

A lead magnet has exactly two jobs:
1. **Convert a visitor into a known contact** (get the email)
2. **Begin the trust-building process** (deliver enough value that they open your next email)

If it only does #1, you have a list of people who will never buy. If it only does #2, you have fans who never opted in. It must do both.

#### The Value-Relevance Matrix

The best lead magnets sit in the top-right quadrant:

```
                    HIGH RELEVANCE TO OFFER
                           │
     Interesting but       │    THE SWEET SPOT
     doesn't lead to       │    High value + directly
     a sale                │    bridges to your offer
                           │
    ───────────────────────┼───────────────────────
                           │
     Waste of time —       │    Useful but
     low value, off topic  │    disconnected from
                           │    what you sell
                           │
                    LOW RELEVANCE TO OFFER
    LOW VALUE ─────────────────────────────── HIGH VALUE
```

**The mistake:** Creating a high-value lead magnet that has zero relationship to what you sell. A great ebook on "10 Time Management Tips" doesn't help sell your FSM software — even if people download it. The lead magnet must bridge to the offer.

#### The Qualification Filter

Not all leads are created equal. The best lead magnets pre-qualify:

| Lead Magnet Type | Who It Attracts | Quality |
|-----------------|----------------|---------|
| Generic ("free ebook on marketing") | Everyone, including people who'll never buy | Low |
| Topic-specific ("HVAC marketing audit") | People with the exact problem you solve | High |
| Tool/calculator ("Revenue leak calculator for service businesses") | People actively trying to solve the problem | Very High |
| Assessment ("Is your business ready for [your solution]?") | People evaluating solutions right now | Highest |

**Rule:** The more specific and action-oriented the lead magnet, the higher the lead quality — even if total volume is lower. 100 qualified leads > 1,000 random emails.

### Phase 2: Concept Generation

Generate 5-7 lead magnet concepts across different format types. Don't default to "ebook" — the format should match the audience's preferred way to consume value.

#### Format Options (Ranked by Perceived Value)

| Format | Perceived Value | Consumption Rate | Creation Effort | Best For |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Interactive tool/calculator** | Very High | Very High | Medium-High | Quantifiable problems, B2B, SaaS |
| **Assessment/audit/quiz** | Very High | Very High | Medium | Consulting, services, agencies |
| **Template/swipe file pack** | High | High | Low-Medium | Creators, marketers, operators |
| **Checklist/scorecard** | High | Very High | Low | Process-oriented audiences |
| **Mini-course (3-5 lessons)** | High | Medium | Medium | Education, coaching, complex topics |
| **Workshop/training recording** | High | Medium | Medium | Education, coaching, expertise-based |
| **Spreadsheet/workbook** | High | Medium-High | Medium | B2B, finance, planning, operations |
| **Report/guide (short: 5-15 pages)** | Medium-High | Medium | Medium | Research-driven, B2B, emerging topics |
| **Cheat sheet / one-pager** | Medium | Very High | Low | Quick reference, tactical audiences |
| **Email course (5-7 day drip)** | Medium | Medium | Medium | Nurture-heavy funnels, complex sales |
| **Ebook (long: 20+ pages)** | Medium-Low | Low | High | Thought leadership (but often unread) |
| **PDF guide (generic)** | Low | Low | Medium | Avoid — oversaturated, low engagement |

**Trend note:** Interactive formats (calculators, assessments, quizzes) consistently outperform static formats (PDFs, ebooks) in both opt-in rate and engagement. If you can make it interactive, do it.

#### Concept Generation Framework

For each concept, define:

```
CONCEPT: [Name]
FORMAT: [Type from table above]
PROMISE: [What the prospect gets — specific outcome in their language]
HOOK: [The one-line pitch that makes them opt in]
CONSUMPTION TIME: [How long to use/read — shorter is usually better]
QUICK WIN: [What specific result they get from consuming this]
BRIDGE TO OFFER: [How this naturally leads to wanting your paid product/service]
CREATION EFFORT: [How hard is this to build?]
```

#### Idea Triggers

If you're stuck, answer these questions to generate concepts:

1. **What's the #1 question your prospects ask before buying?** → Turn the answer into the lead magnet.
2. **What's the first step someone should take before using your product/service?** → Make that step the lead magnet.
3. **What data or insight do your prospects wish they had?** → Package it.
4. **What does your prospect need to BELIEVE before they'll buy?** → Create content that builds that belief.
5. **What task do they hate doing manually that you could automate or templatize?** → Build the tool.
6. **What would a 5-minute version of your paid service look like?** → That's your lead magnet.
7. **What are your competitors' customers complaining about?** → Solve that specific complaint for free.

### Phase 3: Concept Scoring & Selection

Score each concept against criteria that predict conversion AND downstream revenue.

#### Scoring Matrix

| Criteria | Weight | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Opt-in Appeal** | High | Would someone give their email for this without hesitation? Is the perceived value immediately obvious? |
| **Speed to Value** | High | How fast do they get a result? Minutes > hours > days > weeks. |
| **Bridge Strength** | High | How naturally does this lead to wanting the paid offer? (Not forced — natural.) |
| **Lead Quality** | High | Does this attract people likely to buy, or just freebie collectors? |
| **Consumption Rate** | Medium | Will they actually USE it, or download and forget? |
| **Creation Feasibility** | Medium | Can you build this in a reasonable timeframe with available resources? |
| **Shareability** | Low-Medium | Will they tell others about it? (Bonus, not requirement.) |
| **Uniqueness** | Medium | Is this different from what competitors offer for free? |

Score each concept 1-10 on each criterion, weighted by importance. Select the highest scorer.

#### Red Flags to Watch For

- **"I'd download this but never use it"** — High opt-in, zero engagement. Looks good in metrics, worthless for revenue.
- **"This is great but has nothing to do with what they sell"** — Builds goodwill but no purchase intent.
- **"Everyone else has something like this"** — Undifferentiated. Won't stand out in a crowded market.
- **"This would take 3 months to build"** — Scope creep. Start simpler, iterate.

### Phase 4: Lead Magnet Design

#### The Opt-In Page

The opt-in page has one job: convert visitors to subscribers. Keep it ruthlessly focused.

**Elements (in order):**

```
HEADLINE: [Specific outcome they get]
"Find the exact leaks costing you jobs — in 5 minutes."

SUBHEADLINE: [How — format and what's included]
"Answer 15 questions. Get your custom marketing health score instantly."

VISUAL: [Mockup or preview of what they'll receive]
Show them what they're getting. Screenshots, preview images, tool interface.

BULLETS (3-5): [Specific things they'll learn/get/discover]
- Exactly where you're losing leads in your funnel
- Your response time score vs. industry benchmarks  
- 3 quick wins you can implement this week

FORM: [Minimal fields]
Name + Email (maximum). Every additional field reduces conversion.
For some audiences, email only is best.

CTA BUTTON: [Benefit-driven, not "submit"]
"Get My Free Audit" / "Show Me My Score" / "Send Me the Templates"

SOCIAL PROOF (optional): [Below the fold]
"Join 2,400+ service business owners" / Testimonials about the lead magnet itself
```

**Opt-in page rules:**
- No navigation menu (eliminate escape routes)
- No competing CTAs
- Mobile-first design (most traffic is mobile)
- Load fast (every second of load time costs conversions)

#### The Lead Magnet Content

Regardless of format, every lead magnet should follow this structure:

```
1. QUICK CONTEXT (10% of content)
   - Who this is for
   - What they'll walk away with
   - How to use it for best results
   - Set expectations on time investment

2. THE CORE VALUE (80% of content)
   - The tool, templates, frameworks, assessment, or training
   - Must deliver on the promise made on the opt-in page
   - Must provide a genuine quick win
   - Must be ACTIONABLE — not just informational

3. THE BRIDGE (10% of content)
   - "Now that you've [result from lead magnet]..."
   - "The next step is [what your paid offer does]"
   - Soft CTA — not a hard sell, but a clear next step
   - "If you want help implementing this, here's how..."
```

**The bridge is the most important part for revenue.** Without it, you've given away value with no path to monetization. But it must feel natural — not a bait-and-switch.

#### The Thank You / Delivery Page

Don't waste the thank you page. The prospect just took action — they're in momentum.

**Options:**
1. **Tripwire offer** — "Before you go, grab [low-cost offer] for $X" (see tripwire-offer skill)
2. **Booking CTA** — "Want us to walk you through your results? Book a free 15-min call."
3. **Content preview** — "While you're here, watch this 3-minute video on [topic related to lead magnet]"
4. **Community invite** — "Join 500+ [audience type] in our free community"

At minimum, confirm delivery and set expectations: "Check your email in the next 2 minutes for your [lead magnet name]."

### Phase 5: Follow-Up Sequence (Handoff to email-sequences)

The lead magnet starts the relationship. The email sequence builds it. **Do not write the full sequence here** — that's the `email-sequences` skill's job.

#### What This Skill Defines (for handoff)

1. **Trigger event:** Lead magnet downloaded — [specific asset name]
2. **What the lead magnet revealed:** The gap or desire it created (the bridge to your paid offer)
3. **Bridge message:** How free value connects to the paid offer — "Now that you've [result from lead magnet], here's how to [bigger result]"
4. **Ideal timing:** When to introduce the core offer (typically email 3-5, after 2-3 pure value emails)
5. **Sequence type:** Welcome → Nurture (bridge to core offer)
6. **Recommended length:** 5-7 emails, roughly 3:1 value-to-pitch ratio

#### Handoff to email-sequences

Run the `email-sequences` skill with:
- **Trigger:** Lead magnet download
- **Context:** Lead magnet topic, what gap it revealed, what the core offer solves
- **Sequence type:** Welcome → Nurture
- **Brand voice file:** Reference docs/brand-voice.md

The email-sequences skill will handle the full sequence design including behavioral branching, timing, and individual email copy.

### Phase 6: Placement Strategy

Where the lead magnet lives determines how many people see it.

| Placement | Description | Conversion Rate |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Dedicated landing page** | Standalone page, traffic driven via ads/links | 20-50% |
| **Blog post content upgrade** | Contextual opt-in within related content | 5-15% |
| **Exit intent popup** | Triggered when user moves to leave the page | 3-8% |
| **Sticky bar / hello bar** | Persistent banner at top or bottom of site | 1-5% |
| **Sidebar widget** | Persistent opt-in in blog sidebar | 0.5-3% |
| **In-content modal** | Button/link that opens opt-in form mid-content | 5-15% |
| **End of content** | CTA placed after article/page content | 2-8% |

**Best practice:** Use the dedicated landing page for paid traffic. Use content upgrades and modals for organic traffic. Layer multiple placements on your site — not everyone converts on the first touchpoint.

## Inputs Required

To execute this skill, provide:
1. **Business/product description** — What you sell and to whom
2. **Target audience** — Specific description of who you want on your list
3. **Core offer** — What paid product/service will the lead magnet eventually lead to?
4. **Positioning / angles** — If positioning-angles skill has been run, provide output
5. **Existing content / assets** — What do you already have that could be repackaged?
6. **Funnel context** — Where does the lead magnet sit? (Standalone landing page? Blog content upgrade? Ad-driven?)
7. **Competitors' lead magnets** — What are competitors offering for free? (So you can differentiate)
8. **Constraints** — Timeline, technical capabilities, budget for building the asset

## Quality Checks

- [ ] Would the target prospect give their email for this WITHOUT hesitation? (If there's any "meh," it's not compelling enough.)
- [ ] Can they get a meaningful quick win within 15 minutes of consuming it?
- [ ] Does the lead magnet naturally bridge to the paid offer — without feeling like a bait-and-switch?
- [ ] Is the format one the audience will actually consume? (Don't give a 40-page PDF to people who want a quick answer.)
- [ ] Does it pre-qualify leads? (Will people who download this actually be likely to buy?)
- [ ] Is it different from what competitors offer for free? (If everyone has a "marketing guide," yours needs a twist.)
- [ ] Is the opt-in page focused on ONE action with zero distractions?
- [ ] Does the follow-up sequence deliver value before asking for the sale?
- [ ] Would you personally find this valuable if you were the target prospect?
