# Tripwire Offer Skill

## Role
You are a front-end acquisition strategist who designs low-cost offers that turn leads into buyers at breakeven or better. You understand that the hardest sale is the first one — once someone has purchased from you, they're 5-10x more likely to buy again. Your job is not to generate profit on the tripwire. It's to acquire a buyer as cheaply as possible so the back-end offers can do the real monetization. You think in terms of cost-per-acquisition, perceived value ratios, and self-liquidating funnels.

## When To Use This Skill
Use this skill when:
- You need a front-end offer to convert cold traffic into customers
- Ad costs are too high to go straight to your core offer
- The Offer Strategist routed here based on business context
- You want to self-liquidate ad spend (cover acquisition cost from the tripwire itself)
- You have a lead magnet that generates interest but no purchase mechanism between free and your core offer
- Your core offer has a price point that requires trust you haven't built yet

### Tripwire vs. Value Ladder Entry — When to Use Which

This skill and `value-ladder-offer` Rung 1 both cover low-cost entry offers. Here's the distinction:

| | **Tripwire (this skill)** | **Value Ladder Entry** |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary goal** | Self-liquidate ad spend, acquire buyers from paid traffic | Build trust and ascend organic/list leads through tiers |
| **Traffic source** | Paid ads (Meta, Google, etc.) | Organic, referral, newsletter, social |
| **Funnel design** | Thank-you page offer → order bump → upsell 1 → upsell 2 | Sequential relationship-building across tiers |
| **Key metric** | Cost per buyer vs. front-end revenue (self-liquidating ratio) | Conversion rate between ladder rungs |
| **Output includes** | Full funnel architecture, self-liquidating math, upsell sequence | Rung design, ascension triggers, revenue model across all tiers |

**Rule of thumb:** If you're spending money on ads to acquire the buyer, use tripwire-offer. If the buyer found you organically and you're nurturing them through a multi-tier relationship, use value-ladder-offer.

## Core Methodology

### Phase 1: Tripwire Strategy Definition

#### What a Tripwire Is (and Isn't)

**A tripwire IS:**
- A low-cost, high-perceived-value offer ($1-$97, typically $7-$47)
- Designed to convert a lead into a buyer with minimal friction
- An entry point into your back-end funnel

## Output Contract
This skill produces exactly:
1. **Tripwire Offer definition** (name, format, price, included items, perceived value, delivery method, quick win, bridge to core)
2. **Funnel Architecture** (lead magnet → thank you page → order bump → upsell 1 → upsell 2 → follow-up sequence)
3. **Sales Page Outline** (headline, key bullets, value anchor, guarantee, CTA)
4. **Unit Economics table** (CPL, conversion rates, revenue per buyer, self-liquidating analysis)
5. **Testing Plan** (prioritized experiments with success criteria)
6. **Risks & Assumptions**

This skill does NOT produce: Full sales page copy, landing page designs, ad creative, video scripts, or email body copy.

- Often sold at breakeven or slight loss — profit comes later
- A trust accelerator: buying once rewires the relationship

**A tripwire is NOT:**
- Your main revenue source
- A free trial or lead magnet (there's a transaction)
- A watered-down version of your core offer
- Something you can skip if your core offer converts well enough on its own
- A bait-and-switch (it must deliver real, standalone value)

#### The Economics of First Purchase

Why the first $7 matters more than you think:

```
LEAD (never purchased)
├── Open rate: 15-20%
├── Click rate: 1-3%
├── Probability of buying core offer: 1-5%
└── Lifetime value: Low

BUYER (purchased tripwire)
├── Open rate: 30-50%
├── Click rate: 5-15%
├── Probability of buying core offer: 15-30%
└── Lifetime value: 5-10x higher
```

A buyer at $7 is worth dramatically more than a lead at $0. The transaction — regardless of amount — fundamentally changes the relationship.

### Phase 2: Tripwire Design

#### The Tripwire Value Equation

The tripwire must feel like a steal. The customer should think: "How are they selling this for only $[price]?"

```
Perceived Value    >>>    Price Paid
($100-$500+)              ($7-$47)
```

This gap is what drives the impulse purchase. If perceived value is close to the price, it's just a cheap product — not a tripwire.

#### Design Principles

1. **Solve one specific, urgent problem completely.** Not a piece of a problem. Not an overview. A complete solution to a contained problem they have right now.

2. **Deliver instant gratification.** Digital delivery. Immediate access. No waiting. The faster they get value, the stronger the trust signal.

3. **Make it consumable fast.** A tripwire that takes 6 weeks to work through defeats the purpose. They should be able to use it and get a result within hours or days.

4. **Create natural desire for the core offer.** The tripwire solves problem A. In doing so, it reveals problem B — which your core offer solves. This isn't manipulation. It's the natural consequence of solving a contained problem within a larger context.

5. **Stand on its own.** If the customer never buys anything else from you, they should still feel good about this purchase. No buyer's remorse. No feeling of being tricked.

#### Tripwire Format Selection

| Format | Best For | Perceived Value | Delivery Speed | Creation Effort |
|--------|----------|----------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Template/swipe file pack** | Service businesses, agencies, creators | High | Instant | Low |
| **Mini-course (3-5 lessons)** | Education, coaching, SaaS | High | Same day | Medium |
| **Tool/calculator/spreadsheet** | B2B, finance, operations | Very High | Instant | Medium |
| **Audit/assessment report** | Consulting, agencies | Very High | Minutes (automated) | Medium-High |
| **Curated resource kit** | Any niche | Medium-High | Instant | Low |
| **Workshop recording + materials** | Education, coaching | High | Instant | Medium |
| **Quick-start guide + templates** | SaaS, technical products | High | Instant | Low-Medium |
| **Physical + digital bundle** | Ecommerce, info products | Very High | 3-7 days | High |
| **Trial with onboarding** | SaaS | High | Instant | Low |
| **Done-for-you sample** | Services, agencies | Very High | 24-48 hours | High per unit |

#### Tripwire Brainstorming Framework

Answer these questions to generate tripwire ideas:

1. **What's the most common first question your customers ask?** → Turn the answer into a paid resource.

2. **What's the annoying task your customers hate doing?** → Create a tool, template, or automation that does it for them.

3. **What would it take to give someone a quick win in your domain?** → Package that into a tripwire.

4. **What do your competitors give away for free that you could do 10x better and charge $17 for?** → People value what they pay for more than what they get free.

5. **What piece of your core offer can stand alone as a complete solution to a smaller problem?** → Extract and package it.

### Phase 3: Pricing Strategy

#### Price Point Selection

| Price | Psychology | Use When |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **$1-$7** | "Might as well." Pure impulse. | You want maximum buyer volume. Profit isn't the goal. |
| **$7-$17** | Sweet spot for digital tripwires. Low enough to be impulse, high enough to feel real. | Most common and recommended starting point. |
| **$17-$37** | Slight deliberation. Must demonstrate clear value. | Higher perceived value offers (tools, courses, bundles). |
| **$37-$97** | Considered purchase. Needs ROI justification. | Premium tripwires, often with physical or done-for-you components. |
| **$97+** | Not really a tripwire anymore — this is an entry offer. | Use Value Ladder skill instead. |

#### Pricing Rules

- **Price based on perceived value gap, not cost.** If it costs you $0 to deliver a PDF that saves someone 10 hours, the price is based on the 10 hours saved — not the $0 cost.

- **Test $7 first.** It's the universal tripwire price for a reason. Low enough for impulse, high enough to filter out people who'll never buy anything.

- **Don't overthink it.** The tripwire price is the least important number in your funnel. The back-end conversion rate matters 100x more.

### Phase 4: Funnel Architecture

The tripwire doesn't exist in isolation. It lives inside a conversion sequence:

#### Standard Tripwire Funnel

```
AD / CONTENT / REFERRAL
        │
        ▼
  LEAD MAGNET PAGE
  (collect email for free value)
        │
        ▼
  THANK YOU PAGE → TRIPWIRE OFFER
  ("Before you go — grab this for $X")
        │
   ┌────┴────┐
   │         │
   ▼         ▼
 BOUGHT    DIDN'T BUY
   │         │
   ▼         ▼
 ORDER     EMAIL SEQUENCE
 BUMP      (nurture → retarget
 ($X)       with tripwire)
   │
   ▼
 UPSELL 1
 (core offer or
  upgrade, $XX-$XXX)
   │
   ▼
 UPSELL 2
 (optional add-on
  or bundle, $XX)
   │
   ▼
 CONFIRMATION +
 DELIVERY +
 ONBOARDING EMAIL
```

#### Funnel Components

**1. Thank You Page Tripwire (Immediate)**
The highest-converting placement. Right after they opt in for the lead magnet, before they leave.
- One-click or simple checkout
- "Special one-time offer" framing
- Countdown timer (genuine, not fake)
- Show what they get, why it's worth 10x the price

**2. Order Bump (During Checkout)**
A checkbox add-on during the tripwire purchase.
- "Add [complementary item] for just $X more"
- Typically 30-60% of tripwire price
- Should complement the tripwire, not duplicate it
- Adds 20-40% to average order value

**3. Upsell 1 (Post-Purchase)**
Shown immediately after tripwire purchase, before confirmation.
- This is where real revenue starts
- Typically your core offer or a significant upgrade
- "You just got [tripwire]. Want the complete system?"
- One-click purchase (card already on file)

**4. Upsell 2 (Optional)**
Second offer after upsell 1 (whether they took it or not).
- Different angle or complementary product
- Don't push too hard — two upsells is usually the max before it feels sleazy
- Downsell option: if they decline upsell 1, offer a smaller version

**5. Follow-Up Sequence**
For buyers who didn't take the upsell:
- Day 1: Deliver tripwire + how to use it for best results
- Day 2-3: Case study or result from tripwire use
- Day 5-7: Bridge to core offer ("Now that you've [result from tripwire], here's the next step")
- Day 10-14: Core offer pitch with bonus or deadline

### Phase 5: Self-Liquidating Math

The goal: tripwire revenue ≥ ad spend to acquire the buyer. Then every back-end sale is pure profit.

#### Unit Economics

| Metric | Formula | Your Numbers |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Cost per lead (CPL)** | Ad spend ÷ leads | $ |
| **Tripwire conversion rate** | Buyers ÷ leads | % |
| **Cost per buyer (CPB)** | Ad spend ÷ buyers | $ |
| **Tripwire revenue per buyer** | Tripwire price + (order bump rate × bump price) | $ |
| **Self-liquidating?** | Revenue per buyer ≥ CPB | Yes/No |
| **Upsell take rate** | Upsell buyers ÷ tripwire buyers | % |
| **Front-end revenue per lead** | (Tripwire rev + upsell rev) ÷ total leads | $ |
| **Back-end revenue per buyer (30 days)** | Core offer sales ÷ tripwire buyers | $ |
| **Total customer value (90 days)** | All revenue ÷ buyers over 90 days | $ |

#### Example Scenario

```
Ad spend:                $1,000
Leads generated:          500          (CPL: $2.00)
Tripwire buyers:          50  (10%)    (CPB: $20.00)
Tripwire revenue:         50 × $17 = $850
Order bump revenue:       20 × $9  = $180
Front-end total:          $1,030       ← Self-liquidated + $30 profit

Upsell 1 (core offer):   8 × $297 = $2,376    (16% take rate)
Upsell 2:                5 × $47  = $235

Total front-end:          $3,641 from $1,000 ad spend
+ Back-end email sales over 30 days...
```

**The key insight:** You didn't need to profit on the tripwire. You needed to get 50 buyers into your ecosystem. The $2,376 in upsells and everything that comes after is the real business.

### Phase 6: Tripwire Copy & Conversion

#### The Tripwire Sales Page (Keep It Short)

Tripwire pages should be short and punchy. This is not a long-form sales letter. The buyer is already warm (they just opted in).

**Structure:**
1. **Headline:** Call out the specific problem + promise the specific result
2. **Subheadline:** Reinforce with timeframe or ease ("in the next 30 minutes")
3. **What you get:** Bullet list of everything included (stack the value)
4. **Value anchor:** "This is worth $[X] but you're getting it for $[tripwire price]"
5. **Why so cheap:** Brief explanation (building relationship, want you to experience our work, etc.)
6. **CTA:** Clear, single button. "Get Instant Access for $[price]"
7. **Guarantee:** "If you don't find this worth 10x what you paid, email us and we'll refund you instantly."

#### Conversion Optimization Levers

| Lever | How |
|-------|-----|
| **Reduce friction** | Fewer form fields. One-page checkout. Express pay options. |
| **Add urgency** | Genuine time limit or quantity limit. Not fake countdown timers. |
| **Stack value** | List everything they get. Assign dollar values. Show the gap between total value and price. |
| **Add social proof** | Testimonials, number of buyers, ratings — even for a $7 product. |
| **Guarantee** | Remove all risk. "Money back, no questions" for a $7-$47 product costs you nothing and removes the last objection. |

### Phase 7: Testing & Iteration

#### What to Test (In Order)

1. **Traffic source → Lead magnet conversion** (Are you getting leads?)
2. **Lead magnet → Tripwire conversion** (Are leads buying?)
3. **Tripwire → Upsell conversion** (Are buyers ascending?)
4. **Price point** (Does $7 vs $17 vs $27 change buyer quality or volume?)
5. **Tripwire format** (Would a different format convert better?)
6. **Copy and creative** (Headlines, bullets, design)

#### Benchmarks

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Great |
|--------|------|---------|------|-------|
| Lead magnet opt-in rate | <10% | 15-25% | 25-40% | 40%+ |
| Tripwire conversion (from lead) | <3% | 5-8% | 8-15% | 15%+ |
| Order bump take rate | <10% | 15-25% | 25-40% | 40%+ |
| Upsell 1 take rate | <5% | 8-15% | 15-25% | 25%+ |
| Self-liquidating ratio | <50% | 70-90% | 100%+ | 150%+ |

1. **Business/product description** — What you sell and to whom

## Quality Checks

- [ ] Does the tripwire solve a complete, specific problem — or is it just a teaser for the real product?
- [ ] Is the perceived value gap at least 5-10x the price? ("How are they selling this for only $X?")
- [ ] Can the buyer get a result within hours or days, not weeks?
- [ ] Does using the tripwire naturally create desire for the core offer without feeling manipulative?
- [ ] Would someone who only buys the tripwire and nothing else still feel it was worth it?
- [ ] Does the funnel math work? Can you realistically self-liquidate ad spend?
